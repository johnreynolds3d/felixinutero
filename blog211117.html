A-Frame v. Three.js Revisited

21 NOVEMBER 2017

I woke up to an announcement from Mozilla regarding their latest efforts to 'accelerate the arrival of the Social 3D Web'. A few months ago I went through a period of agonizing and tumultuous indecision (basically a daily occurance with me, but this was even worse than usual) regarding which of the popular WebVR libraries I should devote my time to learning.

I spent quite a bit of time experimenting with Babylon.js, which had great support for input controllers, and also built a few demos using A-Frame, but I got stuck trying to get both teleportation and regular input working together.

Today I added a link to my homepage which opens an example scene from Fernando Serrano's 'aframe-input-mapping-component'. I hope he won't mind.
